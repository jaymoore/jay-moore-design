## 0. Reframe — what Phase 0 is FOR (added 2026-05-14, after Codex R5 + Jay's constraint)

**The portfolio is a job-getting instrument first, a craft artifact second.**

Phase 0 originally drifted toward "find the most beautiful direction calibrated to the craft-mentor cohort." That was a luxury framing. Jay's actual situation: job searching, building a not-yet-paying business, bills are real, target is interviews in weeks — not becoming a named designer over 12 months.

Both goals (get hired / become a named designer) overlap, but they are NOT the same goal, and when they conflict, **time-to-hire wins.** Every Phase 0 decision is now tested against one question: *does this get a skeptical recruiter to reply faster?*

Consequence: the "become tomorrow" goal is real and good but it is a **12-month** goal, not this month's. The launchable, hireable version ships now. The named-designer version is what Jay iterates toward AFTER there is a paycheck. Features that serve "become tomorrow" but not "get hired" are deferred to v1.1 — explicitly, on the record, not deleted.

## 2. The bet (REVISED 2026-05-14 — absorbs Codex R5 + the job-getting reframe)

> **An interaction designer who codes. The live, working interface is the native language — demos are the hero object on every page.**
>
> **The site is an artifact, not a frame: simple surface, deep craft underneath. The calm and confidence of one-idea-per-screen, the inspectable rigor that survives a senior reviewer pulling it apart.**
>
> **But calm has a job. The portfolio is a job-getting instrument first. The homepage is a recruiter scan spine — in 45 seconds a skeptical reviewer sees the pivot, sees one hero demo with one-line method proof, and sees exactly where to click for depth. Calm, never slow.**
>
> **Demos are the hero, not the whole story. A thin evidence rail stays visible, pointing to the proof a recruiter scans for: the SPM case study (it has the metrics), the /about pivot bridge, and contact. The thinking shows up as terse, sharp captions beside the work — one sentence, the way Jay already thinks.**
>
> **This compresses the seven-section homepage to a three-block scan spine. Subtraction, not amnesia — every cut is accounted for, not erased.**

## 3. Mentor pattern survey (2026-05-14)

Sources read in depth — landing pages, craft indexes, AND the essays that expose thinking:
rauno.me + /craft + /notes/1 + /craft/interaction-design; emilkowal.ski + "Developing Taste" + "You Don't Need Animations"; paco.me; leerob.com; geoffreylitt.com + "We need AI HUDs". Brian Lovin and Rauchg were rate-limited at fetch time — substituted from known patterns, flagged.

### Common across all craft mentors

- **Brutal text-first restraint.** No hero graphics. No "view my work" buttons. No card grids. The page IS the typography. Rauno's homepage = one sentence + seven imperative fragments. Paco = three short paragraphs. Lee = a paragraph + links.
- **The site is small.** Homepage, a craft/projects index, a writing index, contact. Restraint is the flex.
- **Low content density; whitespace does the work.** Generous line-height, narrow measure, lots of air.
- **Writing is load-bearing for THEM** (Emil 14 essays, Litt 25+, Rauno turns interactions into essays). NOTE: this is how they became NAMED — but that is a 12-month goal, not Jay's get-hired goal. See Section 0 reframe + Codex R5 note in Section 7.
- **Motion is earned, never ambient.** The motion-famous mentors (Emil, Rauno) explicitly teach RESTRAINT. Emil: "You Don't Need Animations." Rauno: high-frequency / low-novelty interactions get ZERO animation. Motion lives in demos and craft pieces, almost never in page chrome.
- **Monospace as a structural accent** — metadata, timestamps, labels, code. Never the body face.
- **Identity stated flat.** One clean noun phrase, no hedging.

### Where they diverge

- **Type personality:** designed-but-restrained with a real hand (Rauno, Paco) → deliberately invisible / near system-default (Lee, Litt). Both respected. No single correct point.
- **Color temperature:** mostly near-monochrome, but warm-white vs clinical cool-gray varies. Accent is nonexistent or used once.
- **Craft surface — the "what's my evidence" answer:** named interaction pieces (Rauno, Paco) vs research projects (Litt) vs essays (Lee). Each picked ONE primary evidence type and went deep.

### The key finding

The spec's Section 6 is a competent AVERAGE of this cohort (stone gray, Geist, Perfect Fourth, hairline borders, restrained motion, mono-for-metadata). Every instinct in it is correct AND shared by all the mentors — which is the problem. The mentors don't look like each other; each metabolized the shared values and made a SPECIFIC BET. The spec inherited the shared values and skipped the bet. Shared values = table stakes, necessary but not sufficient, not differentiation. Phase 0's job is to add the bet — BUT see Section 0: the bet serves get-hired, not craft-cohort applause.

### Jay's calibration signals (from the conversation)

- Resonates MOST with Rauno — "a designer that codes," interaction design as long-held specialization.
- Respects Paco's craft but voice "too diminished" — RULES OUT invisible-on-purpose. Wants craft with a point of view.
- Pulled to thedot.space for **calm** (space, one idea per screen) and **image-forward** confidence — NOT for its marketing-site execution (Squarespace app landing page; the genuinely good part is the dot-grid motif used as a structural system).
- "Become tomorrow" is real — but it is the 12-month goal, not this month's. Risk: tribute-act / single-mentor gravity (Rauno). Mitigation: anti-derivative constraints, Section 4.
- Wants work described as "simple, beautiful, easy to understand" — clarified: **simple surface over deep craft**, not minimal-all-the-way-down.
- Headline evidence = live interaction demos (hero object, not sole evidence).
- "Not a writer" + no time to write during job search + business build. Long-form writing deferred to v1.1. Voice at v1 = terse captions beside demos.
- **Bills are real. Time-to-hire is the constraint.** This reframed all of Phase 0 — see Section 0.

### Round 2 — REVISED after Codex Round 6. The structure lock round.

**Codex Round 6 verdict: Round 2 plan as originally written was NOT SAFE — revision required.** Two compounding problems: (a) it multiplied the riskiest build (three novel hero interactions in one round = scope self-sabotage), and (b) it had no lock criterion, so Round 2 might not end Phase 0. Both fixed below without changing the direction choice. Full verdict logged in Section 7.

**Same root cause as Codex Round 5.** R5 caught the seven-section homepage as recruiter-anxiety dressed as craft. R6 caught the three-novel-heroes plan as the same instinct relocated one level down — when in doubt, multiply. The checkpoint exists to catch exactly this. It did. The revised plan isolates variables instead of multiplying them.

**Not three new directions. Not three new heroes. Three iterations within Instrument that vary CHROME around ONE fixed hero.**

**Fixed across all three iterations (the variables deliberately held still):**
- **ONE thesis-demonstrating hero interaction.** Built once, identical in all three iterations. The decision-support / trust-surface interaction — the AI staying legible to the person. The hero stops being a variable so the structural moves can actually be judged. (Codex R6 #2 — the single most important change.)
- **Light mode as a first-class designed system** — NOT a dark inversion. Designed from scratch as the primary state. (Codex R6 #5.)
- **Type and color held at Direction C's current values** — Hanken Grotesk / JetBrains Mono / signal-blue on near-black-inverted-to-light. Held still *on purpose* so structure is the only thing moving. These get their dedicated critical-quality attention in Round 3 — they are NOT folded into Round 2 and NOT shipped as-is.
- 3-block scan spine, vertical scroll, **no section boxes**, Jay-specific copy (SPM metrics, decision-support framing, Fiserv bridge), terse one-sentence captions, thin always-visible evidence rail, mono as structural accent, dark-mode toggle, responsive, inspectable, real CSS.

**What actually varies — chrome density around the fixed hero:**
1. **Iteration 1 — HUD-on.** Persistent HUD stays, but every field runs the decision-relevance filter: if a field doesn't change a recruiter's decision in 45 seconds, it's deleted. Realistically that leaves ~one proof-bearing field (a verifiable process/evidence link) and little else. (Codex R6 #1, #4.)
2. **Iteration 2 — HUD-pared.** HUD concept survives but folds into the nav — instrumentation as a quiet line in the persistent chrome, not a separate bar.

The control that tests whether the HUD earns its place. Jay already flagged the HUD "cliché and overused" — Codex R6 #4 read that as a kill signal; this iteration treats the HUD as a hypothesis under test, not a foundation. If HUD-on and HUD-pared don't measurably beat the control on scan speed + credibility, the HUD dies.

Each iteration also carries a **different hairline-grid intensity**, so grid gets calibrated in passing.

- **Conflict winner, declared up front:** when rigor and calm fight — *calm wins on the homepage scan spine* (it is the recruiter instrument), *rigor wins inside the demo* (it is the proof).

- b. Instrument identity **survives light-default** without leaning on cliché chrome.
